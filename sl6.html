Saint Anthony of Padua

Saint Anthony's missionHe was probably canonized because of the many miracles he performed, and that his life was devoted to God. He even gave up visits from his family so that he could focus on his studies about God. Someone who was around when he was said: "Anthony would preach, teach and confess all day long, until sunset. Often, he would never eat all day long." Both of those made him definitely merit sainthood.

Canonized by who??

Saint Anthony was canonized by Pope Gregory IX on 30 May 1232, at Spoleto, Italy. It was less than one year after his death, which shows a lot! Plus, in 1946 he was proclaimed Doctor of the Church by Pope Pius XII, due to how good his sermons were.

Virtues?

Saint Anthony practiced many virtues. He understood the love for God as the supreme good and to love people for the sake of God. He also lived the basic values of poverty, obedience, and humility, considering the latter as the root of all other virtues. He seems to have lived like that for at least most of his life.

Feast Day

Saint Anthony's feast day is the 13th of June. I will try to remember to practice it, and I hope you do too!

PatronageSaint Anthony is a patron saint of a lot of things! The highlights are Lisbon, lost items/people/souls (more on that later), amputees (remember that miracle from earlier?), animals, elderly people, mail, poor/oppressed people (remember that miracle about the poor woman?), pregnant women, Portugal, shipwrecks (remember? his ship wrecked on Sicily?), starvation, infertility, and a lot more.
If you're wondering about the lost items, it's probably because of yet another miracle. This one is where someone steals Saint Anthony's psalm book. Anthony prays, and the thief decides to give the book back.
Items associated with Saint AnthonyOne item associated with Anthony is the tongue. This is due to a legend about him, in which 30 years after he died his body withered away, except for the tongue which still looked like it was alive. This was most likely a sign of his preaching gift. There is also a prayer dedicated to Saint Anthony, that goes something like Saint Anthony, Saint Anthony, please come around. Something is lost and must be found."
If Saint Anthony were in the world todayIf Saint Anthony was in the world right now, I think he'd be really sad at how much poverty and how little virtues in the world there are, and how we're trashing the Earth. I think that he'd continue his sermons, but that not many people would listen because everybody would be glued to their phones. It's my sad opinion.
How is he still important to the church?Saint Anthony is a model for Christains today because of what he did. His mission was stunning - he gave up so much for so little in return. This is what we need to do. We need to get off of our addictive devices and help people! It's our only chance of having a decent, equitable planet.
Things that impress me about Saint Anthony
The first thing that impresses me about his life is one legend where people didn't listen to his preaching. He went to the shoreline and started preaching; a great amount of fish grouped up to hear his preaching. This and another legend involving a mule that bowed to the Eucharist is probably why he was proclaimed the patron saint of animals. There is also another legend where he realised the food that was put before him was poisoned. His hosts admitted to them trying to murder him. They then told him that if he really believed Mark 16:18 ("...and if they drink any deadly thing, it will not harm them") he should by all means eat the food. Anthony blessed the food, and ate it, and the hosts were astonished to see that he was perfectly fine. The second thing that impresses me about his life is something way after his life - being chosen by a Pope as Doctor of the Church in 1946. That's quite a compliment. And the third thing (but certainly not the least important) is that he gave up his parents' fortune, to live a life like Jesus'. That's a really good thing to do, and I was surprised when I read that. I do have more things that I was impressed about - basically every facet of his life - but that would take too long to write.

How can I follow Saint Anthony's example in my everyday life?To follow Saint Anthony's example in my everyday life, I can stop being so greedy. I can continue making public websites like these to spread the word of God, and donate to good causes (like Elive, the Internet Archive, and charities). I could turn away large amounts of profit in return for just enough. Things like that, I could do. It would be hard, but possible.
